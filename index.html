Welcome!

    I am a PhD candidate working in condensed matter theory with Prof. Michael Hermele.

    I was born in Hualien, Taiwan. I got my bachelor's degree in Physics at Tsing-Hua University in Hsinchu. The first thing that inspire me toward condensed matter physics is the plateau of integer quantum Hall effect from the general physics course taught by Prof. Hsiu-Hau Lin. The amazingly accurate behavior is now understood as the composite effect of non-trivial band topology and impurities. Since then, I am obsessed to those quantum emergent phenomena (Of course, I don't understand this term before I came to Boulder.).

    After that, I have an oppertunity to join the research group of Prof. Daw-Wei Wang to work on projects related to ultracold atoms. Our work focusing on the collective behavior of quasi one dimensional ultracold dipolar fermion. I learned invaluable knowledge through the interaction with Prof. Daw-Wei Wang.

    In the fall 2010, I arrive CU Boulder. And keep working on the quantum emergent phenomena with Prof. Michael Hermele from the study of strong spin-orbit interaction and emergent SU(N) alkaline earth atoms.
